 Gratitude Forester’s Home for Abandoned Tieflings. Mx Forester is a lvl 20+ Tiefling [possibly multiclassed] who has retired from adventuring by founding a home for what could be charitably called their people. You are all toddler Tieflings with a desperate want of candy and no means to legally acquire it. As toddlers, you only have access to one cantrip, as well as any racial traits. Your mission is to reach the store on the corner, and steal some candy!

 Your stats are all 10, with application of racial modifiers and 4 hit points [unless you have a con boost, in which case you have 5]. Damage dealt to players deals 1hp. When players hit zero, they take a nap-nap [and are caught and sent back to the Home]
     
    Your items are all toys it could be reasonable for a toddler to have [eg: pull-along duck, squeaky hammer, little red wagon, teddy bear, etc] one toy per player
     
    The goal is to steal 1 pound of candy for each player
     
    Players should be encouraged to do cute widdle babbie chawacter voices

In-journey hazards: [pick three or roll 1d10 until you get three]

1 - Big Friendly Dog - knocks the toddlers over or scare the beans out of them [dex or int save] can be defeated by a game of fetch

2 - Crossing the Road - wis check to see if they know how to cross safely

3 - Neighbour(s) -  charisma check to charm their way past 1d4 neighbours who are pretty certain these babies shouldn’t be out on their own, unsupervised

4 - Mrs Chalmsley’s Pet - pick any kind of D&D pet that could plausibly scare a baby [eg: Pseudodragon, big cat] roll animal handling[wis] to get past

5 - Stairs - a huge [to toddlers] flight of stairs which will need a dex roll to combat - and some enginuity if the team is bringing along the red wagon

6 - Witchy Pu - The gnarled old shopkeeper of the bookstore down the street, who has to be a witch because they always have this broom that they could whack a toddler with [stealth to get past?]

7 - Temmug Pinch-cheek - townsperson who thinks these babies are just TOO adorable and will pinch their widdle cheeks and deal damage.

8 - Nurse/Nanny - one of the caregivers at the Home, will definitely pick up the kids and take them back to the Home if they’re spotted.

9 - Stinky Rubbish - con save to get past without gagging on the fumes and therefore not wanting candy any more

0 - Muddy Puddle - will save to avoid splashing in it and therefore rolling disadvantage on future charisma checks

Final Boss is Mr Muddlewonk and the 1d10+1 customers already in the candy store, and whatever other hazards might be in there. [Each has 5hp and there is no murdering in this game]

Candy [takes 1 action to consume, and players can still use their movement on  that turn.]

     
    Lollipops: if you consume a lollipop you can regain 2 hp. (5 lollipops = pound, you may grab 3 per turn)
     
    Gumballs: gumballs can be spilled on the floor, functioning like a bag of ball bearings. (20 gumballs = a pound, using gumballs wastes half a pound, you may grab 5 gumballs per turn) 
     
    Chocolate: Invokes Sugar Rush. (2 chocolate = a pound, you may grab 1 per turn, 1 chocolate = one sugar rush)
     - Sugar Rush gives player(s) advantage on all strength and dexterity checks for five minutes (since initiative hopefully will not be a factor, if it is invoked in a combat situation, the duration is 1 minute), however after, player must make a DC: 15 con save to avoid falling asleep for a post-sugar nap.

Archetypes: Players can choose or roll 1d8 to select their archetype, thereby cementing the school of magic they can have their one cantrip from:

1 - Attac or Pwotec [Abjuration]

2 - Makit Happen [Conjuration]

3 - Sneaky Peekie [Divination]

4 - Cutie Patootie [Enchantment]

5 - Agent of Chaos [Evocation]

6 - Pwetender [Illusion]

7 - Big Big Naptime [Necromancy]

8 - Pwesto Change-o [Transmutation]

*The Cantrip known as Toll The Dead is, for this game, renamed Toll The Tired, and plays a soothing lullabye to send the opposition to sleepy-byes. Similarly, Spare the Dying is now called Wakey Wakey.

We playtested this at home, and some thought might be put into adding obstacles to get the team to actually escape the Home. I used the hook that the players were all "betht fwiendth"

PLAYER RULES

    You are all tiefling toddlers, each pick a different subrace
    All your stats are 10, then apply your racial boosts.
    you have 4 hp (5 if you have the con boost) if you reach zero, you are unconscious. 
    Your only abilities are your racial traits.
    You have broken out of the orphanage with no money, and seek a treasure trove of candy from the store. 
    How you retrieve this candy, without being apprehended, is up to you.

 DM RULES

    Any environmental or effect that could potentially hurt a toddler will only deal 1 hp of damage.
    The candy store functions as a dungeon:
        the shopkeep is a final boss (objectively has 5 hp if they go the combat route, however, this would only cause him to fall unconcious— there will be no toddler murder.)
        other patrons are to be befriended or avoided. 
            Any creature that is not considered Charmed by the toddlers, whether by magic or exceptional persuasion checks and baby tief cuteness, will report any thievery they see.
        Environmental hazards can include
            gumballs on the floor
            getting distracted by a cute kitty or dog
            the candy is on the top shelf!!! just out of reach!
    To reach your goal:
        you must steal a pound of candy for each player character. you may also steal excess to aid you in your heist just be careful how much you use.
    Candy
        Lollipops: if you consume a lollipop you can regain 2 hp. (5 lollipops = pound, you may grab 3 at once)
        Gumballs: gumballs can be spilled on the floor, functioning like a bag of ball bearings. (20 gumballs = a pound, using gumballs wastes half a pound, you may grab 5 gumballs at once) 
        Chocolate: Invokes Sugar Rush. (2 chocolate = a pound, you may grab 1 at once, 1 chocolate = one sugar rush)
            Sugar Rush gives you advantage on all strength and dexterity checks for five minutes (since initiative hopefully will not be a factor, if it is invoked in a combat situation, the duration is 1 minute), however after, you must make a DC: 15 con save to avoid falling asleep for a post-sugar nap. 
    You may design your candy store as you will for maximum chaos
    roll a d10 + 1 for the number of potential patrons within.
