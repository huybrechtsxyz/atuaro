[h2]Rule of Cool[/h2]


[h2]On Feats[/h2]
When building your character you do not use feats. Feats are given by the DM for playing a certain style. You can of course talk to your DM. 

You always have the grappler feat from the moment you reach level 2.

[h2]Action Point[/h2]
Players reach a milestone after every two encounters. The DM determines if an encounter counts towards the milestone. The milestone count is reset after a long rest. So to reach a milestone, you must fulfill two encounters before you take a long rest. You reach additional milestones for the fourth, sixth, eighth and so on, encounters which you complete without an extended rest. For every milestone you reach, you gain an additional action point. You can also choose to have one extra use of one magic item's power or one extra spell slot. The milestone defines the type of The spell slot that is regained, and is cumulative. You can convert them into a lower slot with a 1 on 1 ratio. You lose these when taking a long rest.
1 st milestone : 1 level 1 slot
2 nd milestone : 1 level 1 slot, 1 level 2 slot
3 th milestone : 1 level 1 slot, 1 level 2 slot, 1 level 3 slot
...

[h2]On Being Prone[/h2]
When a creature gets knocked prone it can stand up at the cost of half its movement. It grants advantage on attacks, has disadvantage on its attacks and saves, and has no reactions that turn. If a creature goes prone by choice, it can get up with half movement only.

[h2]On Being Disarmed[/h2]
A creature can use a weapon attack to knock a weapon or another item from a target's grasp. The attacker makes an attack roll contested by the target's Strength (Athletics) check or Dexterity (Acrobatics) check. If the attacker wins the contest, the attack causes no damage or other ill effect, but the defender drops the item, unless your ability defines otherwise.

The attacker has disadvantage on its attack roll if the target is holding the item with two or more hands. The target has advantage on its ability check if it is larger than the attacking creature, or disadvantage if it is smaller.

The target can pick up the weapon with the usual rules but It grants advantage on attacks, has disadvantage on its attacks and saves, and has no reactions that turn. If a creature drops an item by choice these rules do not apply.

[h2]On Switching Weapons[/h2]
Main weapons, with a maximum of 2.
A creature can switch between his main weapons a single free action. This can be done once, either at the start or the end of its turn.

[h2]Throwing a weapon.[/h2]
You can take a ‘readied’ weapon like a dagger or handaxe, if it has the throwable property, while holding your current weapon in a single hand as a single free action.

For other weapons or items, the basic rules for drawing and sheathing are both counted as full free actions, so they cannot be done on the same turn. To get around this, the player must drop the weapon in their hand, and draw the alternate weapon.

When using two-weapon-fighting, if the player wants to stow the original weapon, they switch between their main weapons, if reasonable. A skill check can be required if the DM calls for it when switching weapons in any condition.

[h2]On Flanking[/h2]
You receive a penalty to AC for the flanked target. An -1 for every flanker with a max of +2.
A character can not assist in flanking if it is being flanked itself.

[h2]On Casting[/h2]
Ranged spells have disadvantage when next to an enemy combatant. Save spells grant advantage. Any melee combatant has advantage on their next attack.

[h2]On Grabbing[/h2]
You always have the grappler feat (from level 2).

[h2]Combat Conditions[/h2]
The lingering marks of combat and physical pain affect your appearance and actions. People can read the signs of bodily damage and may act caring or aggressive. Realistic and engaging narration of the effects from combat should be rewarded with Inspiration Points.

[h3]Bruised[/h3]
When you lose 1/4 of your Max HP, you are bruised. Your body or face is covered with visible scratches and bruises, and you have minor lingering pains. Anyone can recognize exposed damage with a DC 10 Perception check. Hiding it or using makeup can add +5 or +10 to the DC.

[h3]Bloodied[/h3]
When you lose 1/2 of your Max HP, you are bloodied. Your body has open wounds or massive hematomas, and you feel intense pain during physical feats requiring Strength or Dexterity. Even if your wounds are hidden, anyone can recognize the signs of pain with a DC 10 Perception check.

[h3]Beaten[/h3]
When you drop below 1/4 of your Max HP, you are beaten. Your body is a mess of bleeding open wounds, cracked or splintered bones, or much worse. You feel severe pain just by simply moving around. No check required.


[h2]Conditions[/h2]
[h3]Exhausted (aka Exhaustion)[/h3]
While	you	are	subjected	to	the	Exhausted Condition	(known	in	older	books	as	Exhaustion),	you	experience	the	following	effects: Levels	of	Exhaustion. This	Condition	is	cumulative.	Each	time	you	receive	it,	you	gain	1	level	of	exhaustion.	You	die	if	your	exhaustion	level	exceeds 10. 

[b]Every d20	rolls is affected.[/b]
When you make any d20 Test, you subtract your exhaustion level x 2 from the d20 roll. This include but is not limited to ability checks, attack roles,   Spell Save DCs. Subtract your exhaustion level x 2 from the Spell Save DC of any Spell you cast.

[b]Ending the Condition.[/b]
Finishing	a	Long	Restremoves	1	of	your	levels	of	exhaustion.	When your	exhaustion	level	reaches	0,	you	are	no longer	Exhausted.	

[h3]Grappled[/h3]
While	you	are	Grappled,	you	experience	the	following	effects:
[b]Speed	0.[/b] Your Speed is 0 and can’t change.
[b]Attacks	Affected.[/b] You have Disadvantage on Attack Rolls against any target other than the grappler.
[b]Movable.[/b] The	grappler can drag or carry you, but the grappler suffers the Slowed Condition while moving, unless you are Tiny or two or more Sizes smaller than the grappler
[b]Escape.[/b] While Grappled, you can make a Dexterity or Strength Saving Throw against the grapple’s escape DC at the end of each of your turns, ending the Condition on yourself on a success. The Condition also ends if the grappler is Incapacitated or if something moves you outside the grapple's range without using your Speed.

[h2]Critical Hit and Fumble[/h2]
Critical hits and fumbles happen when you roll a natural 20 or 1 on a 20-sided die. This means you rolled a 1 or 20 without any modifiers like your attack mod (but rolling a natural 20 with advantage counts). There is a seperate table for weapons and spells.

When a creature suffers a minor injury it can be healed by the spell lesser restoration (or same or higher level healing spells) or by using the recuperating downtime activity (Player's Handbook, page 187). Major injuries will not heal on their own and require a heal or a greater restoration spell to heal.

If an effect cannot be applied, roll again until you roll an effect that can be applied.

[h2]Disarm Action[/h2]
[h3]Weapon attack[/h3]
A creature can use a weapon attack to knock a weapon or another item from a target's grasp. The attacker makes an attack roll contested by the target's Strength (Athletics) check or Dexterity (Acrobatics) check. The attacker has disadvantage on its attack roll if the target is holding the item with two or more hands. The target has advantage on its ability check if it is larger than the attacking creature, or disadvantage if it is smaller.

If the attacker wins the contest, the attack causes no damage or other ill effect, but the target drops the item in the immediate vicinity. If the target later picks up the weapon while within 5 ft. of the disarming attacker, all applicable allies may take an attack of opportunity against the target.

[h3]Magical Attack[/h3]
A creature can use a spell attack to disrupt the target's spellcasting for a round. The attacker makes a spell attack roll contested by the target's spellcasting check (using their spellcasting ability modifier). The attacker has advantage on its attack roll if it uses the same ability to cast spells as the target (e.g. Intelligence attacker and Intelligence target). The attacker has disadvantage on its attack roll if the target uses a different spellcasting ability.

If the attacker wins the contest, the attack causes no damage or other ill effect, but the target cannot cast either a cantrip or a spell that uses a spell slot/points until the end of their next turn.
