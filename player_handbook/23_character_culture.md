## About Heritages
The heritage you are raised in is as important to your development as the family you are born into. While your chosen heritage usually describes how your character grew up, the heritage you choose could also represent the environment in which they spent a number of their formative years.

The heritage you choose grants traits that have been learned, trained, or otherwise developed over time. For example, shadow elves lead very different lives from high elves, who live different lives from their wood elf counterparts. You can choose any heritage, even one usually associated with another heritage. When choosing a heritage that is dramatically different from your heritage, you should always ask why, in order to further develop your character’s origin story. You can choose only one heritage during this step.

Some heritages are commonly associated with certain lineages. There are forest gnome and wood elf communities which may share a geographical root, but are manifestly different in social structure, custom, or tradition. Similarly, deep dwarf and deep gnome communities are very different. A human who grew up in a forest might come from a wildling village, or have been brought up in a wood elf tree-town or a secretive forest gnome enclave. Dwarves can be adopted by elvish communities, and gnomes can live their entire lives in grand cosmopolitan cities. While heritages have suggested heritages, this should not be viewed as a rule or limitation, but merely as an indication that particular combinations are common.

Each heritage contains a range of traits. You gain all of the traits associated with your chosen heritage, unless the text says otherwise.
